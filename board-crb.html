Civil Rights Board

Reviews allegations and violations of the Non-Discrimination and Fair Housing Ordinances. Makes recommendations to the City on policies and practices that lead to discrimination. Established September 2020 under Chapter 18 of the Code of Ordinances.

The Civil Rights Board implements the general policy and purposes set forth in Chapter 18 "Non-Discrimination" and Chapter 18A "Fair Housing" of the City of Kalamazoo Code of Ordinances. The Board was created in September 2020 following updates to Chapter 18.

The Board fosters mutual understanding and respect among all people in the City, working to discourage and prevent unlawful discriminatory practices toward the protected classes described in the Ordinance.

  • Review claims of discrimination brought by City residents and make recommendations for resolution following investigation by the City Manager or designee
  • Review and analyze conditions, practices, attitudes, and policies within Kalamazoo that contribute to discriminatory impact and further inequities
  • Make recommendations to the City Commission and City Manager on ways to improve programs and ordinances to eliminate discrimination
  • Engage in public awareness activities to ensure residents understand local protections and the role of the Board
  • Engage with federal, state, and local agencies to help address issues of discrimination in the City
  • Submit an annual report to the City Commission regarding complaints received and actions taken by February 1 of each year

The Board consists of 9 members — 7 voting members and 2 ex-officio members who may fully participate in meetings but do not vote.

Appointment
Voting members are appointed by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission. Members must demonstrate experience in civil rights, public accommodation, employment, disability, or housing law.
Expertise Required
Experience in civil rights, public accommodation, employment, disability, or housing law.
Residency
Members are generally required to be City residents. Up to 2 of the 7 voting seats may be filled by non-residents — such as someone who works in the City or has special expertise. Non-resident members hold full voting rights.
Ex-Officio Members
One City Commissioner and one City officer or employee recommended by the City Manager — both appointed by the Mayor.

Civil Rights Board meetings are open to the public — no registration required. Meetings are held the first Wednesday of every other month at 5:00 PM in City Commission Chambers at Kalamazoo City Hall, 241 W South Street.

Public Comment: Citizens may address the Board for up to 4 minutes. Sign in at the start of the meeting before 5:00 PM. Written comments may also be submitted to the staff liaison in advance of a meeting.

Minutes & Agendas: Meeting records are published on the City's Civic Clerk portal. Scroll up on the portal page to browse past meeting agendas, minutes, and supporting documents.

Interested in Serving? Board seats are filled through mayoral appointment with City Commission approval. Use the Apply to Serve page to submit an interest form or learn more about the application process.

Following investigation by the City Manager's office, the Board reviews findings and makes recommendations for resolution — giving residents a direct path to accountability.
