The Kalamazoo Municipal Golf Association oversees the operations of the City's three public golf courses — Red Arrow, Milham Park, and Eastern Hills. The Board adopts an annual budget, sets greens and membership fees, and establishes rules and regulations for the grounds and the behavior of members.

The Association is responsible to the City Commission of the City of Kalamazoo, Michigan.

Membership in the Association is open to any person irrespective of age, race, sex, or religion, upon payment of annual dues.

The Board of Governors consists of members elected by the Association, two City Commission appointees, and three ex-officio City officials — all of whom hold voting privileges.

Elected Members
Members of the Association elected at each Annual Meeting. Two members are elected annually to serve three-year terms. The majority of elected members must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo.
City Commission Appointees
Two City of Kalamazoo residents with a demonstrated interest or background in golf, turf management, finance, or accounting, appointed by the City Commission.
City Manager or Designee
Ex-officio member with voting privileges, serving by virtue of holding the office of City Manager.
Parks & Recreation Director or Designee
Ex-officio member with voting privileges, serving by virtue of holding the office of Director of Parks and Recreation.
Finance Director or Designee
Ex-officio member with voting privileges, serving by virtue of holding the office of Director of Finance.
Term Limits
Elected and appointed members may not serve more than two consecutive three-year terms.
  • To be the final authority upon all matters affecting the Association and the golf courses.
  • To enact and enforce rules and regulations for the use of the golf courses and for the conduct of members.
  • To control and direct expenses and have management of the practical working of the Association. Any proposal to expend money requires the affirmative vote of at least one ex-officio member.
  • To appoint any special committee considered necessary.
  • To adopt a budget for one year at the regular monthly meeting in February; the budget is approved by the City Commission.
  • To recommend greens fees and membership fees to the City Commission each November for the following year.
  • To ensure that an annual audit is performed of the Association's financial affairs.
