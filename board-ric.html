Investment Committee of the Retirement System & Perpetual Care Investment Committee

Two unified investment oversight committees sharing membership and meeting schedule — together overseeing the long-term investment of both the City's employee retirement fund and its perpetual care fund assets.

The Investment Committee of the Retirement System (RIC) and the Perpetual Care Investment Committee (PCIC) are two distinct advisory committees of the City of Kalamazoo that operate with shared membership and a unified meeting schedule — meeting jointly as a single body on a quarterly basis.

The RIC oversees the investment of assets held within the City's employee retirement and pension system, ensuring the long-term financial health of the City's defined benefit plan for City employees and retirees.

The PCIC oversees the investment of the City's perpetual care funds — trust assets dedicated to the ongoing maintenance of the City's municipal cemetery and other perpetual care obligations.

By sharing membership and meeting jointly, both committees function as a unified investment governance body providing coordinated oversight across two distinct public fund portfolios.

Each committee consists of seven members who serve concurrently on both the RIC and PCIC. Members are appointed by the City Commission and serve three-year terms with no term limits.

Committee Size
Seven members serve on both the RIC and PCIC simultaneously, providing unified oversight across both City investment portfolios.
Term Length
Members serve three-year terms, providing continuity in investment oversight while allowing for regular renewal of committee membership.
Term Limits
There are no term limits. Members may be reappointed for successive terms, allowing the committees to retain experienced investment knowledge over time.
Appointment
Members are appointed by the City Commission. City residency is not required — the committees draw on investment and finance professionals from the broader region.
Meeting Frequency
Both committees meet jointly on a quarterly basis at the W.E. Upjohn Institute for Employment Research, 300 S Westnedge Ave, Kalamazoo.
Staff Liaison
The City's Chief Financial Officer serves as staff liaison, supporting administrative operations and providing financial analysis to both committees. The CFO is not a voting member.

As the joint investment oversight body for both the City's retirement system and its perpetual care fund, the committees are responsible for the following:

  • Reviewing investment performance reports and quarterly portfolio updates for both fund portfolios
  • Evaluating current investment managers and strategies, and recommending changes as appropriate
  • Ensuring compliance with the City's investment policies for retirement and perpetual care funds
  • Monitoring asset allocation and risk exposure across both portfolios
  • Providing fiduciary oversight of the City's major public trust fund investments
  • Advising the City Commission and staff on investment matters related to the retirement system and perpetual care obligations
