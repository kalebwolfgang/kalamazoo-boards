Board of Review for Assessments

Hears citizen appeals regarding property assessments. Has authority to raise or lower assessed values in accordance with state law and evidence presented. Meets in March for appeal hearings, and in July and December to approve corrections to the Assessment Roll.

March Hearings

The Board of Review for Assessments hears citizen appeals regarding property assessments made by the City Assessor's office. The Board has the authority to raise or lower the assessed value of properties based on evidence presented and in accordance with the Michigan General Property Tax Act.

Each year prior to the March sessions, the City Assessor mails a "Notice of Assessment, Taxable Valuation, and Property Classification" to property owners. This notice includes the State Equalized Value, any Principal Residence or Qualified Agricultural Property exemption percentage, and ownership transfer information.

The Board meets for several weeks in March to hear property assessment appeals, beginning on the first Tuesday after the first Monday of March. It also convenes in July and December to approve corrections to the Assessment Roll.

Pursuant to the City of Kalamazoo Charter and Code of Ordinances and the Michigan General Property Tax Act, the Board of Review meets in March to consider:

  • Review and hear protests on the Assessment Roll as prepared by the City Assessor, including protests as to Assessed Value (SEV), Tentative Taxable Value, and Assessment Classification
  • Receive and consider homestead poverty exemption claims
  • Hear appeals related to property valuation and assessment roll classification changes
  • Approve corrections to the Assessment Roll during July and December sessions
  • Any other business that may properly come before the Board

Note: Taxable value changes resulting solely from Proposal A are generally not appealable before the Board of Review unless there has been new construction or a transfer of ownership.

The Board consists of 9 members appointed by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission.

Appointment
Members are appointed by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission.
Term Length
Members serve 3-year terms.
Residency
All members must be City Residents.
Alternates
The Board includes one Alternate member who may serve in place of a regular member when needed.

In-Person Hearings — By Appointment Only. Walk-ins are not permitted for the March appeal sessions. To schedule an in-person appointment, call 269-337-8845 before 5:00 PM on the scheduling deadline.

Written Protests are accepted from both residents and non-residents. Submit a signed letter with a completed Form L-4035 and any supporting documents via:

  • Mail: City Assessor, 241 West South St, Kalamazoo, MI 49007
  • Email: [email protected]
  • Drop Box: 24-hour drop box at Kalamazoo City Hall

Written appeals must be received by the City Assessor no later than 12:00 PM on the final session date. Postmarks are not acceptable. If representing a property owner, written authorization must be provided.

Accommodations: Persons with disabilities should contact the City Assessor's office at [email protected] or (269) 337-8636 by 4:00 PM the day prior to the meeting.

Minutes & Agendas: Meeting records are published on the City's Civic Clerk portal.

Serve on the Board of Review for Assessments

The Board of Review for Assessments needs City residents with knowledge of property valuation, real estate assessment, or local tax law. Members are appointed by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission to serve 3-year terms. Submit an interest form through the City's application portal to be considered for open or upcoming vacancies.
