Downtown Development Authority Board

The Downtown Development Authority (DDA) is a special purpose local authority established under Michigan's Downtown Development Authority Act (Public Act 57 of 2018). Its purpose is to halt deterioration of property values and to foster economic growth in Kalamazoo's downtown business district. DDA activities are funded through revenue from a Tax Increment Financing (TIF) district and a 2-mill property tax levy on properties within the Downtown Development District.

The DDA Board works in close coordination with the Downtown Economic Growth Authority (DEGA) — a related authority established by the Kalamazoo City Commission in October 2018 under the Corridor Improvement Authority (CIA) Act. Both boards share the same membership and meet on the same schedule, though each operates under separate statutory authority with its own development and TIF plan. DEGA is empowered to assist with improvements and investments along commercial corridors within its district boundaries.

The DDA Board consists of 11 members: the Mayor of the City of Kalamazoo (serving as an ex-officio member) and 10 appointed members. Members are appointed by the Mayor with the approval of the City Commission. All members serve without compensation but are reimbursed for actual and necessary expenses.

Property Interest Requirement
A majority of the 10 appointed members must have an interest in property located in the downtown district, or be officers, trustees, members, principals, or employees of a legal entity with such an interest.
Residency Requirement
At least one member must be a resident of the downtown district, provided 100 or more persons reside within the district boundaries.
Term Length
Members serve 4-year terms, limited to 2 consecutive terms. After serving 2 consecutive terms, members must take a 4-year break before being eligible for reappointment.
Appointment
Appointed by the Mayor of the City of Kalamazoo with the approval of the City Commission. The Mayor serves as an ex-officio voting member of the Board.
Officers
A Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and Secretary-Treasurer are elected by the Board at the annual meeting in March. Officers serve 1-year terms and may be re-elected.
Vacancies
Members whose terms have expired continue to serve as holdovers until a successor is appointed. Vacancies created by resignation or removal must be filled within 30 days.

Under Public Act 57 of 2018, the DDA Board is authorized to:

  • Prepare economic analyses and study the impact of metropolitan growth on the downtown district
  • Develop long-range plans to halt property value deterioration and promote economic growth in the downtown district
  • Plan, propose, and implement construction, renovation, rehabilitation, and restoration of public facilities and existing buildings
  • Make and enter into contracts necessary or incidental to the exercise of its powers and duties
  • Acquire, own, convey, and lease land and property within the downtown district as reasonably necessary to its purposes
  • Fix, charge, and collect fees, rents, and charges for the use of Authority-controlled buildings or property
  • Accept grants and donations of property, labor, or other items of value from public or private sources
  • Create, operate, and fund marketing initiatives that benefit retail and general marketing of the downtown district
  • Contract for broadband service and wireless technology in the downtown district
  • Establish and operate loan programs to fund improvements for existing downtown buildings to make them marketable for sale or lease
  • Create, operate, and fund retail business incubators in the downtown district

Serve on the Downtown Development Authority Board

The DDA Board seeks members with a stake in Kalamazoo's downtown district — particularly those with an interest in downtown property, business, or economic development. A majority of members must have an interest in downtown district property. Members are appointed by the Mayor with City Commission approval to 4-year terms.
