The Historic District Commission (HDC) is a quasi-judicial body established under Michigan Public Act 169 of 1970 and City Ordinance No. 986. The Commission conducts design review hearings to decide issues relating to the enforcement of the Historic District ordinance, and to permit — within the standards established by the ordinance — exceptions to strict compliance within recognized historic districts and as applied to designated landmarks.

The HDC plays a critical role in preserving Kalamazoo's built heritage by ensuring that alterations to structures within historic districts maintain the character and integrity of those areas. The Commission coordinates the planning and development of historic districts citywide and serves as an agent of the City Commission in accepting and administering grants and gifts for historic preservation purposes.

The Commission meets on the third Tuesday of each month at 5:00 p.m. in the City Commission Chambers at City Hall. Officers — a Chair and Vice Chair — are elected by the Commission at the Annual Meeting held each February.

All commissioners must be City of Kalamazoo residents and are appointed by the City Commission. The Commission has two distinct qualification requirements that apply to its composition as a whole.

City Residency
All commissioners must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo at the time of appointment and throughout their service.
Architect Requirement
At least one commissioner must be a graduate of an accredited school of architecture, have two years of architectural experience, or be a duly registered architect in the State of Michigan.
Historic Preservation Interest
A majority of commissioners must have a clearly demonstrated interest in historic preservation. This ensures the Commission can evaluate design review decisions with appropriate sensitivity to historic character.
City Commission Appointment
All commissioners are appointed by action of the City Commission following a formal application and review process.
Term Length
Commissioners serve 3-year terms. Members may serve two consecutive terms before a required 3-year break. Officers serve one-year terms and may be re-elected.
Conflict of Interest
Any commissioner with a personal interest in a matter before the Commission must disclose that interest and may be disqualified from voting, as recorded in the minutes.

The HDC exercises its authority under Michigan Public Act 169 of 1970 and City Ordinance No. 986. Its jurisdiction includes the following duties and powers:

  • Regulate the construction, alteration, repair, moving, and demolition of structures in designated historic districts and for designated landmarks
  • Issue certificates of approval or rejection for plans affecting the exterior appearance of historic structures in historic districts
  • Coordinate the planning and development of historic districts in the City of Kalamazoo
  • Recommend capital improvement projects to the City Manager designed to implement the public portions of historic district plans
  • In cases where preservation efforts fail, recommend the City Commission acquire the property if public ownership is deemed most suitable
  • Accept and administer grants and gifts for historic preservation purposes as an agent of the City Commission
  • Maintain public historic structures using funds allocated by the City Commission for this purpose
  • Conduct all proceedings under Robert's Rules of Order; decisions require a majority of a quorum for administrative matters and a full majority of members for substantive actions

Serve on the Historic District Commission

The HDC has open seats for City of Kalamazoo residents who care about preserving Kalamazoo's architectural and historic character. The Commission is currently seeking applicants to fill its required Architect seat — open to graduates of an accredited architecture program, those with two years of architectural experience, or registered architects in Michigan. Residents with a demonstrated interest in historic preservation are also encouraged to apply.
