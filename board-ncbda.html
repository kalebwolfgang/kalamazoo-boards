Northside Cultural Business District Authority Board

Created to support Northside resident-owned businesses and expand business ownership in the neighborhood; build the cultural identity of the neighborhood and preserve significant places; and improve infrastructure, facades, and streets.

The Northside Cultural Business District Authority was established under Public Act 280 of 2005 and Public Act 57 of 2018 to act as a corridor improvement authority for the Northside Neighborhood in Kalamazoo. The Authority works to strengthen the neighborhood's economic vitality, cultural identity, and quality of life.

The Authority's strategic goals include:

  • To provide financial incentives to increase the number of resident-owned businesses, especially those by underserved, underrepresented, historically disenfranchised, historically excluded, and low-income residents.
  • To preserve existing housing and build new housing to accommodate all resident needs.
  • To make enjoyment of the arts, culture, and open space part of the Northside Neighborhood way of life.
  • To strengthen support systems for workforce development, safety, and youth programming.
Board Size
Nine members: eight appointed members and one designated seat for the Mayor of the City of Kalamazoo (or designee).
Term Length
Four-year terms. The Mayor's seat is a designated seat and is not subject to the appointment computation.
Appointment
Appointed members are nominated by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission.
Business Interest
A majority of appointed members must have an ownership or business interest in property located in the development area.
Residency
At least 75% of appointed members must be residents of the Northside Neighborhood. At least one member must live within ½ mile of the development area.
Property Ownership
At least 25% of appointed members must be property owners within the District.

  • Develop long-range plans designed to halt the deterioration of property values and promote economic growth in the development area.
  • Implement development plans in the development area in accordance with Public Act 280 of 2005 and successor acts.
  • Conduct market research and public relations campaigns; develop, coordinate, and conduct retail and institutional promotions; and sponsor special events and related activities.
  • Accept grants and donations of property, labor, or other things of value from public or private sources.
  • Acquire and construct public facilities within the development area.
  • Make and enter into contracts necessary or incidental to the exercise of its powers and performance of its duties.

The Northside Cultural Business District Authority Board meets monthly on the third Thursday of each month at 6:00 PM. All meetings are open to the public in accordance with the Michigan Open Meetings Act.

Meetings are held at the Northside Association for Community Development, 612 N Park St, Kalamazoo, MI 49007, unless otherwise noted. Members of the public are welcome to attend and may address the Board during the public comment period held before adjournment (up to 3 minutes per speaker).
