The Economic Development Corporation (EDC) is a Michigan nonprofit corporation organized under Act 338 of 1974 and established by the City of Kalamazoo to promote economic growth, support business development, and attract investment to the community. The EDC works to strengthen the local economy by facilitating development projects, providing technical assistance to businesses, and administering programs that support both new and existing enterprises.

The EDC operates in close coordination with the Brownfield Redevelopment Authority (BRA) — all EDC members simultaneously serve on the BRA Board. While the EDC focuses on broader economic development tools under Act 338, the BRA administers brownfield plans and uses tax increment financing (TIF) to remediate and redevelop contaminated or blighted properties under Act 381 of 1996. Both entities share membership, staff support, and a unified commitment to sustainable economic growth in Kalamazoo.

The EDC Board consists of 9 to 13 members, providing flexibility to accommodate the needs of the City's economic development activities. Members are appointed by the Mayor of the City of Kalamazoo with the consent of the City Commission. All members serve without compensation.

Residency Requirement
A majority of members must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo. Non-residents are eligible for membership if they are affiliated with businesses operating within the city.
Term Length
Members serve 6-year terms. The bylaws do not specify a limit on consecutive terms, allowing members to be reappointed.
Appointment
Appointed by the Mayor of the City of Kalamazoo with the consent of the City Commission. One City Commission member serves as a liaison representative to the Board.
Officers
A Chairperson, Vice Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer are elected by the Board at the annual meeting held in the third quarter. No officer may hold the same office for more than 3 consecutive terms. The same person may hold two offices.
Quorum
A quorum consists of a majority of the members then in office. Board action requires a majority vote of those present at a meeting where a quorum exists.
Dual Service
All EDC members simultaneously serve on the Brownfield Redevelopment Authority (BRA) Board, enabling coordinated oversight of the City's economic development and brownfield programs.

Under Act 338 of 1974, the EDC Board is authorized to:

  • Promote and facilitate economic development and business activity within the City of Kalamazoo
  • Acquire, own, and transfer real and personal property in furtherance of economic development objectives
  • Borrow money and issue obligations in accordance with the Act to fund development activities
  • Enter into contracts, agreements, and leases necessary to carry out its purposes
  • Accept grants, donations, and contributions from public agencies and private sources
  • Prepare and administer Brownfield Plans in coordination with the Brownfield Redevelopment Authority
  • Provide financial assistance, loans, and other support to eligible businesses and development projects
  • Cooperate with federal, state, and local agencies to advance economic development goals
  • Form committees of the Board by majority vote to assist in carrying out Board functions

Serve on the Economic Development Corporation Board

The EDC Board seeks members with experience in business, economic development, finance, or related fields who are committed to strengthening Kalamazoo's economy. A majority of members must be City residents. Service on the EDC also includes membership on the Brownfield Redevelopment Authority Board. Members are appointed by the Mayor with City Commission consent to 6-year terms.
