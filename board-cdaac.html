The Community Development Act Advisory Committee (CDAAC) is established by and serves in an advisory capacity to the Kalamazoo City Commission for the allocation of Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) public service grants. CDAAC reviews funding applications from community organizations, evaluates them against the needs of the city's low- and moderate-income residents, and makes formal recommendations to the City Commission on how federal HUD resources should be distributed.

CDAAC's work is grounded in the City's Master Plan and the 5-Year Consolidated Plan. The committee ensures that CDBG and HOME funds are directed toward programs that address housing, community development, public services, and neighborhood reinvestment — particularly in the city's federally designated CDBG-eligible neighborhoods.

  • Work with Community Planning and Economic Development (CPED) staff on the annual allocation of CDBG public service dollar grants
  • Align grant allocations with the City Master Plan and the strategies and priorities of the 5-Year Consolidated Plan
  • Understand the public service needs of low- to moderate-income residents to make informed funding decisions
  • Conduct the annual competitive application process to allocate CDBG public service funds not designated for City use
  • Make recommendations to the City Commission on applications reviewed through the competitive process
  • Promote public service activities throughout the city, with special attention to core neighborhoods and areas with concentrations of low- and moderate-income households
  • Annually meet with public service grantees to review and evaluate program performance and understand any obstacles to meeting required outcomes
  • Perform other duties and functions as assigned by the City Commission

CDAAC is composed of 9 City of Kalamazoo residents. The committee includes one representative from each core CDBG-eligible neighborhood that has an active neighborhood association with 501(c)(3) status, with the remaining seats filled at-large.

If a neighborhood association loses its 501(c)(3) status, that seat temporarily converts to an at-large seat until status is reinstated. Neighborhood associations recommend candidates when a neighborhood vacancy occurs, subject to City Commission approval.

Appointment
All members are appointed by the City Commission. CDAAC makes recommendations to the City Commission based on community diversity, relevant expertise, and equitable representation.
Term Length
Members serve 3-year terms (April 1 – March 31), limited to 2 consecutive terms. After 2 consecutive terms, members must wait one full term (3 years) before reapplying. Members filling a vacated seat's remaining time are still eligible for 2 full additional terms.
Residency
All members must be City of Kalamazoo residents.
Areas of Expertise
Members are selected for relevant expertise, which may include: financing, real estate, non-profit management, business ownership, education, special needs services, emergency shelters, homeless assistance, housing rehabilitation, neighborhood job creation, and urban development.
Attendance
A seat is deemed vacated if a member is absent from more than 50% of regular meetings within any 6-month period. Members unable to attend must notify CPED staff or the Chairperson by 2:00 p.m. on the day of the meeting.
Officers
A Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson are elected by active members at the regular June meeting each year. Officers serve 1-year terms and may be re-elected.

CDAAC holds 6 to 7 regular meetings per calendar year, typically on the fourth Thursday of select months (January, February, June, August, September, October, and November) at 5:30 p.m. in the Community Room at City Hall. A quorum requires a simple majority of active members.

Members receive agenda packets — including minutes, relevant documents, and meeting details — at least 5 days before each meeting. All CDAAC documents are subject to disclosure under the Michigan Freedom of Information Act.

During the annual grant cycle, CDAAC conducts a competitive application process through which community organizations may apply for CDBG public service funding. CDAAC evaluates applications and makes formal recommendations to the City Commission, which retains final authority over fund allocations. Community organizations seeking grant funding can apply through the City's HUD Grant Funding page.

Serve on the Community Development Act Advisory Committee

CDAAC seeks City of Kalamazoo residents with expertise in areas such as real estate, non-profit management, housing, urban development, education, or business. Neighborhood representatives are nominated by their neighborhood association; at-large members apply directly. All members are appointed by the City Commission to 3-year terms.
