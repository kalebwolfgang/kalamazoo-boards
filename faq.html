Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Kalamazoo's boards and commissions — what they are, who can serve, and how to apply.

Boards and commissions are citizen bodies created by the City Commission to assist in the administration of city government. Members are Kalamazoo residents appointed to serve on a volunteer basis.

Membership requirements, term lengths, and appointment authority vary by board. All appointments are made by the Mayor or City Commission. Interested residents may apply online or contact the City Clerk's Office.

Advisory BoardsMost boards make formal recommendations to the City Commission or City Manager. The Commission retains final decision-making authority.
Quasi-Judicial BoardsSome boards — including the Zoning Board of Appeals and Building Board of Appeals — make binding decisions on appeals and variances.
ResidencyMost boards require City of Kalamazoo residency. Some permit non-residents where specific expertise is needed. Requirements are listed on each board's page.
ExpertiseNo professional credentials are required for most boards. Several boards seek members with both subject matter expertise and lived community experience.
ConflictsMembers must disclose conflicts on individual agenda items. Certain boards have specific disqualifying conditions listed in their bylaws.
Term LimitsMost boards allow up to two consecutive terms. Members who complete two terms are typically eligible to reapply after a waiting period of one to three years.
CompensationBoard and commission members serve on a volunteer basis and receive no compensation.

The members table columns

DotA colored circle representing the seat's current status (open, transitioning, or seated).
NameThe appointed member's name. It may include a role subtitle (like Chair or Vice Chair) when relevant.
TypeSpecifies the seat category, such as Voting, Ex-Officio, At-Large, Neighborhood Rep, or City Commissioner.
ResidencyIndicates what residency status is being tracked for the seat (e.g., City resident vs. Non-resident).
Term EndThe specific date an active term concludes. "Permanent" indicates no term limit, and "By office held" means the term is tied directly to another position.
Status BadgeA quick visual indicator defining if the seat is Open, Transitioning, or Seated.

Seat status explained

OpenThe seat is entirely vacant with no current member serving in that capacity.
TransitioningThe member's term has either expired or ends within 6 months. They are typically still serving as a holdover pending reappointment or replacement.
SeatedThe member is currently active and serving securely within their appointed term dates.

Key terminology

HoldoverA member whose official term has expired but continues to legally serve and vote because the vacancy process takes time and a successor hasn't been appointed yet.
Ex-officioA member who serves on a board by virtue of holding another specific office, rather than by direct appointment to the board itself.
Term limit vs. term endThe "term end" is the exact date a specific term expires. The "term limit" is the maximum number of consecutive terms a member is legally allowed to serve.
Permanent appointmentAn appointment with no fixed expiration date; the member serves indefinitely (often applied to certain ex-officio seats).
