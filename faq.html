Common questions about Kalamazoo's boards and commissions — what they are, who can serve, and how to apply.

Boards and commissions are citizen bodies created by the City Commission to assist in the administration of city government. Members are Kalamazoo residents appointed to serve on a volunteer basis.

Membership requirements, term lengths, and appointment authority vary by board. All appointments are made by the Mayor or City Commission. Interested residents may apply online or contact the City Clerk's Office.

Advisory Boards Most boards make formal recommendations to the City Commission or City Manager. The Commission retains final decision-making authority.
Quasi-Judicial Boards Some boards — including the Zoning Board of Appeals and Building Board of Appeals — make binding decisions on appeals and variances.
Residency Most boards require City of Kalamazoo residency. Some permit non-residents where specific expertise is needed. Requirements are listed on each board's page.
Expertise No professional credentials are required for most boards. Several boards seek members with both subject matter expertise and lived community experience.
Conflicts Members must disclose conflicts on individual agenda items. Certain boards have specific disqualifying conditions listed in their bylaws.
Term Limits Most boards allow up to two consecutive terms. Members who complete two terms are typically eligible to reapply after a waiting period of one to three years.
Compensation Board and commission members serve on a volunteer basis and receive no compensation.

Meeting frequency

Most boards meet once per month. Some meet quarterly. Specific schedules are on each board's page.

Typical meeting length

Most regular meetings run one to two hours. Hearings may run longer depending on the agenda.

3 yr

Standard term length

Most appointments are for three-year terms. Partial terms are sometimes available mid-cycle.

Meeting format

Most meetings are in person at City Hall. Some boards permit remote participation. Format is noted on each board's page.

Clerk's Office review

Staff review applications for eligibility and forward qualified applications to the Mayor's office or, for some boards, directly to the City Manager.

4

Mayor's recommendation & Commission vote

The Mayor recommends a candidate and the City Commission votes at a public meeting, which is open to attend.

If appointed, your name is read at the meeting

Notification & onboarding

Appointed members are contacted by the Clerk's Office and connected with their board's staff liaison for scheduling and orientation materials.
