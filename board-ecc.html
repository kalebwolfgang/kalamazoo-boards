The Environmental Concerns Committee (ECC) monitors significant environmental trends affecting the City of Kalamazoo and advises the City Manager and City Commission on environmental matters. Established by the City Commission, the ECC plays a critical advisory role — gathering community input and expertise on sustainability, natural resource stewardship, energy, and related environmental topics.

The Committee also serves as a citizens' forum for environmental issues, providing a venue for community members to raise concerns, engage in public discussion, and participate in shaping the City's environmental priorities. Members bring a range of professional and civic expertise, and all must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo.

Meetings are held on the third Wednesday of each month at 4:30 p.m. in the City Commission Chambers at City Hall, and are streamed live on the City's YouTube channel and Facebook page.

ECC membership is open to any qualifying City of Kalamazoo resident. All members are appointed by the City Commission and are expected to actively participate in committee meetings and discussions.

City Residency
Must be a resident of the City of Kalamazoo at the time of appointment and throughout their term of service.
Environmental Interest or Expertise
Must have demonstrated interest and/or expertise in environmental issues — including but not limited to sustainability, natural resources, energy, or conservation.
Written Application
Must complete a written application and file it with the ECC. Applications are reviewed prior to City Commission consideration.
City Commission Approval
All members must be approved by action of the City Commission before serving on the Committee.
Term Limits
Members serve 3-year terms and may serve two consecutive terms. After two consecutive terms, a 3-year break is required before reapplying.
Meeting Attendance
Members are expected to attend all scheduled meetings. Missing more than 3 meetings per year may result in status change to Associate Member or a recommendation for removal.

The ECC is an advisory body established by the City Commission. Its primary duties include:

  • Monitoring significant environmental trends affecting the City of Kalamazoo and the broader region
  • Advising the City Manager and City Commission on environmental policy, planning, and sustainability matters
  • Serving as a citizens' forum where residents can raise environmental concerns and engage in public discussion
  • Gathering and evaluating community input on topics including natural resource management, energy, water quality, urban ecology, and related subjects
  • Making recommendations to City leadership on environmental initiatives and programs
  • Electing a Chair and Vice Chair annually in January by plurality vote of the membership
  • Operating under a quorum requirement — a majority of current members must be present for official action to be taken
