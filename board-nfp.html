Natural Features Protection Review Board

Provides expert knowledge on natural features, development, and construction to assist staff during the Site Plan Review process. Approval from the Board is necessary for a project to obtain full site plan review approval.

The purpose of the Natural Features Protection Review Board is to assist staff in the Site Plan Review process by providing expert knowledge on relevant natural features and development or construction. Approval from the Board is necessary for a project to obtain full site plan review approval as part of the development, permitting, and construction process.

The intent of the Board is to guide development of land in the Natural Features Protection Overlay District in order to protect natural features in the City. The action items in the 2025 Master Plan require the City to work collaboratively with the community for long-term protection of natural features for such ends as public enjoyment and environmental health.

Board Size
Seven members.
Term Length
Three-year terms.
Appointment
Appointed by the Mayor and approved by the City Commission.
Term Limits
No more than two successive terms. Eligible for reappointment after an absence of one term.
Residency
No residency requirement. Members do not need to be City of Kalamazoo residents.
Qualifications
The Board must have at least one member with knowledge in each of: wetlands/water resources, plants/trees, slopes, and site development/building construction.
  • To review development site plans on parcels located in the Natural Features Protection Overlay District to ensure all requirements and standards are satisfied.
  • To serve as an advisory group as the City conducts an analysis of natural features in the City.
  • To work with the City on providing environmental education and stewardship opportunities for property owners, stakeholders, and conservation-minded organizations to protect natural features throughout the City.
