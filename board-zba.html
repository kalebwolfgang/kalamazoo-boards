The Zoning Board of Appeals (ZBA) hears requests for variances from the Zoning Ordinance. The ZBA also provides interpretations of the Zoning Ordinance and hears appeals regarding administrative zoning decisions.

The ZBA was established by the City of Kalamazoo as authorized by the Michigan Zoning Enabling Act (MCL §125.3101 et seq), under the City of Kalamazoo Code of Ordinances, Appendix A – Zoning Ordinance, §11.3.

Board Size
Seven regular members and two alternate members, appointed by the City Commission following ZBA recommendation.
Term Length
Three-year terms. Members may not serve more than two consecutive terms, excluding fulfilling the unexpired term of a former member.
Residency
All members must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo and serve without compensation.
Representation
ZBA endeavors to ensure membership represents a cross-section of the community regarding gender, race, neighborhood composition, and relevant subject-matter expertise.
Quorum
Four members constitute a quorum. A minimum of four affirmative votes is required to approve any request.
Officers
Chair, Vice-Chair, and Secretary are elected by ZBA at its annual meeting in February and serve one-year terms.
  • Hear and decide requests for variances from the Zoning Ordinance.
  • Provide interpretations of the Zoning Ordinance.
  • Hear and decide appeals of administrative zoning decisions made by the City Planner or other city administrative officials.
  • Affirm, modify, or reverse orders, decisions, determinations, or interpretations of the City Planner or city officials, by concurring vote of a majority of ZBA members.

Serve on the Zoning Board of Appeals

ZBA seeks members with experience or interest in zoning, land use, real estate, or community development. All members must be City of Kalamazoo residents. ZBA endeavors to represent a cross-section of the community regarding gender, race, and neighborhood composition.
