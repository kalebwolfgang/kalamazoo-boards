Citizens Public Safety Review and Appeal Board

Provides independent community oversight of the Kalamazoo Department of Public Safety. Hears appeals of complaint investigations conducted by the Office of Professional Standards and advises the City Manager on policies, procedures, and community relations.

The Citizens Public Safety Review and Appeal Board (CPSRAB) provides an opportunity for community members to participate in the oversight of the Kalamazoo Department of Public Safety (KDPS). Created by the City Manager pursuant to the authority granted by the Kalamazoo City Charter, the Board receives citizen input on appeals filed after KDPS complaint investigations and can inquire into any conduct of a public safety officer in the absence of a complaint.

The Board serves three primary purposes: building community trust and credibility in the public safety complaint process; providing an independent review of complaint dispositions that citizens choose to appeal; and advising the City Manager on improvements to policies, procedures, and community outreach related to the complaint process.

For more information about the Board, its work, and annual reports, visit the Board's independent website: kalamazoocpsrab.org.

Review

  • Receive copies of PCR complaints (with personal information redacted) upon request
  • Be updated monthly on the progress of all active complaint investigations by the Office of Professional Standards
  • Inquire about any complaint investigation, regardless of the Chief's finding, in the absence of a formal appeal
  • Review the complaint reporting process and recommend improvements to the Chief and City Manager

Advisory

  • Monitor the overall fairness of the complaint process for both citizens and public safety employees
  • Recommend improvements to the City Manager on policies and procedures implicated in the complaint process
  • Assist the Department of Public Safety in preparing community outreach materials about the complaint process
  • Establish subcommittees to research and address public safety and community relations issues

Reporting

  • Prepare an annual review covering the number and types of complaints, and assess the thoroughness, accountability, and responsiveness of the complaint process
  • Issue reports on police/community relations, racial profiling, and other community climate concerns as needed

Community Outreach

  • Hold meetings with community leaders and neighborhood associations at least annually
  • Organize public forums to discuss community climate and develop action agendas with the Public Safety Chief
  • Work with KDPS and community organizations to anticipate and prevent problems through data analysis and recommendations

The Board consists of up to 12 members appointed by the City Manager — up to 6 from diverse organizations representing diverse populations and up to 6 at-large members. A quorum requires 51% of active members present.

Appointment
All members are appointed by the City Manager. The Chair is named annually by the City Manager; the Vice Chair is recommended by the Chair and approved by the City Manager.
Term Length
Members serve 3-year terms, limited to 2 consecutive terms. A 1-year hiatus is required before reappointment. Leave of absence of up to 3 months may be requested.
Residency
Full-time City of Kalamazoo residents receive first priority. Non-residents with identifiable interests located in the City receive second priority.
Time Commitment
Approximately 5–8 hours per calendar quarter, including monthly meetings, two KDPS ride-alongs or hiring events per year, and two community outreach events per year.
Disqualifiers
Members may not: hold political office or be an announced candidate; be a current City employee; work for another law enforcement agency; be a retired or former law enforcement officer; be an immediate family member of any KDPS personnel; or be under a criminal sentence or active protection order.
Orientation
Members must attend an orientation session within their first 90 days covering public safety policies, the complaint process, use of force, and officer welfare topics. Attendance at the Kalamazoo Regional Citizens Academy is required within the first year of service.

CPSRAB reviews appeals of complaint findings made by the Chief of Public Safety after investigation by the Office of Professional Standards. The Board may only recommend changes to findings of exonerated, unfounded, or not sustained — it cannot impose discipline or review sustained findings.

1
File a Complaint
Submit a Public Safety Community Relations (PCR) complaint with KDPS online, in person, by mail, phone, or email. The Office of Professional Standards (OPS) receives and investigates the complaint.
2
OPS Investigation
OPS investigates within 45 days. Progress updates are provided at least every 45 days. Upon completion, the Chief of Public Safety makes a final finding and notifies the complainant within 10 business days.
3
File an Appeal
If unsatisfied with the Chief's finding, file an appeal with the City Manager within 15 business days of receiving the decision. Appeals may be filed online, in person, by mail, phone, or email.
4
CPSRAB Hearing
The City Manager refers qualifying appeals to CPSRAB. A hearing is scheduled within 30 business days and held within 60. The complainant may appear in person; hearings may be closed to the public at the complainant's request.
5
Board Recommendation
The Board deliberates in closed session and submits a written report to the City Manager within 14 business days recommending to sustain or reverse the Chief's finding. The City Manager retains final authority over the outcome.

Serve on the Citizens Public Safety Review and Appeal Board

The CPSRAB seeks community members committed to equitable public safety and community trust. Members are appointed by the City Manager. City residents receive first priority; non-residents with identifiable interests in the city are also eligible. Members serve 3-year terms with a maximum of two consecutive terms.

Appeal a Public Safety Decision

Qualifying appeals are referred to CPSRAB for an independent hearing. Visit the CPSRAB website for full appeal instructions.
