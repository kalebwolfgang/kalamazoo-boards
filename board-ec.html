The Kalamazoo City Election Commission is a statutory body composed of three city officials: the City Clerk (serving as Chairperson), the City Attorney, and the City Assessor. Members serve on the Commission by virtue of the office they hold — there are no appointed members and no application process. Terms begin upon each official's appointment to their respective city position.

The Commission is responsible for carrying out election-related duties as specified in Michigan state law. Unlike boards with regular monthly schedules, the Election Commission meets as needed in advance of each election — typically twice per election cycle: once to appoint election inspectors and once to conduct the public accuracy test for voting equipment. All meetings are open to the public in accordance with the Michigan Open Meetings Act (Public Act 267 of 1976).

Meetings are governed by Robert's Rules of Order (Newly Revised, most current edition). Special meetings may be called by the Chairperson or any two members with at least 18 hours of public notice. The City Clerk prepares all agendas and meeting materials.

The Election Commission carries out its responsibilities through two categories of action: duties that require a formal open meeting, and administrative duties that may be delegated to the City Clerk by resolution.

Requires Open Meeting
  • Approving ballots
  • Appointing election inspectors (including AVCB members, Receiving Board members, and precinct chair- and assistant chairpersons)
  • Conducting the Public Accuracy Test for voting equipment
  • Approving precinct boundary changes and temporary consolidations
  • Adopting resolutions outlining delegated duties
  • Establishing Absent Voter Counting Boards (AVCBs)
May Be Delegated to City Clerk
  • Preparing meeting materials (ballot proofs, inspector appointment lists)
  • Preparing, printing, and delivering ballots for municipal elections
  • Providing ballot proof copies to candidates and the Secretary of State
  • Notifying voters of precinct changes or consolidations
  • Providing election supplies and ballot containers
  • Preliminary logic and accuracy testing
  • Notifying political parties of certified precinct inspector appointments (federal and state elections only)
