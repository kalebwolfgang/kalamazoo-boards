The Historic Preservation Commission (HPC) is an advisory body whose mission is to work toward the preservation of Kalamazoo's historic resources through advocacy of effective preservation practices, public education, and research, compilation, publication, and maintenance of historic resources inventories.

The HPC advises the City Commission on historic preservation issues — including the establishment of historic districts and landmarks and their nomination to the National Register of Historic Places or the Michigan State Register of Historic Sites. The Commission encourages and promotes historic preservation through publications, public education, the collection of historical materials, and the identification of historical resources that warrant preservation.

The Commission meets on the second Wednesday of each month at 6:00 p.m. at City Hall. Officers — a Chair and Vice Chair — are elected by the Commission at its annual meeting held each February. The City Liaison (Historic Preservation Coordinator) serves as the Secretary/Treasurer and supports all Commission operations.

Note: The HPC is an advisory body distinct from the Historic District Commission (HDC), which holds quasi-judicial design review authority over structures in designated historic districts.

All commissioners are appointed by the City Commission. The HPC has residency and expertise requirements that apply to its membership as a whole.

City Residency
A majority of commissioners must be residents of the City of Kalamazoo. Individual commissioners may be non-residents, provided the resident majority requirement is met.
Historic Preservation Interest
A majority of commissioners must have a demonstrated interest or knowledge of historic preservation or Kalamazoo's history. Expertise in architecture, planning, history, or archaeology is valued.
City Commission Appointment
All commissioners are appointed by the City Commission following a formal application and review process. The City Liaison conducts elections of officers at the annual meeting.
Term Limits
Commissioners serve 3-year terms and may serve two consecutive terms. After two consecutive terms, a 3-year break is required before reapplying for appointment.
Meeting Attendance
A member's seat is deemed vacated if they accumulate more than 50% unexcused absences in any half-calendar year. Absences may be excused if the City Liaison is notified before the meeting.
Conflict of Interest
Any commissioner with a personal interest in a matter before the Commission must disclose that interest and is disqualified from both discussion and voting on that matter.

The HPC is an advisory body to the City Commission. Its authority and principal functions include:

  • Advise the City Commission on all matters relating to historic preservation policy in Kalamazoo
  • Recommend the establishment of historic districts and individual landmarks for local designation
  • Nominate historic properties to the National Register of Historic Places and the Michigan State Register of Historic Sites
  • Research, compile, publish, and maintain historic resources inventories identifying properties that warrant preservation consideration
  • Encourage and promote preservation through public education, publications, and community engagement
  • Appoint subcommittees from Commission members or interested community members to assist with specific responsibilities
  • Take action by affirmative vote of at least 3 members, or a majority of members present, whichever is greater — with a quorum of 5 required
  • Conduct all meetings under Robert's Rules of Order; elect Chair and Vice Chair at the annual February meeting

Serve on the Historic Preservation Commission

The HPC welcomes City of Kalamazoo residents who have a demonstrated interest or knowledge of historic preservation or Kalamazoo's history. A majority of commissioners must be City residents. Backgrounds in architecture, planning, local history, archaeology, or community advocacy are especially valuable to the Commission's work.
